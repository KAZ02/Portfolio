HOKKAIDO

Located in the northernmost of Japan's four main islands, Hokkaido offers many attractions that can only be experienced in Hokkaido, such as its vast nature, culture that differs from that of Honshu, abundant food, and hot springs.

【Abashiri Prison Museum】
This is the northernmost prison in Japan, where prisoners were gathered from all over Japan to cultivate Hokkaido under the slogan "national prosperity and defence" set by the Meiji government. Visitors can learn about the prison and its history at the museum, and at the prison cafeteria, they can eat prison food, which is a reproduction of the lunch that prisoners ate in those days.

【Shiretoko Nature Tour】
Shiretoko National Park,located on the Shiretoko Peninsula in eastern Hokkaido, is one of Japan's beautiful national parks. The peninsula is home to a variety of wildlife, including brown bears, deer and foxes. In winter the sea of Okhotsk wrapped up by drift ice.

【Goryokaku】
Goryokaku was Japan's first Western-style castle and was designed on the model of the "castle cities" that were being developed in Europe at the time. The name "Goryokaku" (meaning "five ridge walls") was given to the fort because of its five ridge walls, which are characteristic of the European-style earthen mound structure. During the Hakodate War, which began in 1868, Goryokaku became the headquarters of the provisional government established by Takeaki Enomoto and other former shogunate vassals.
